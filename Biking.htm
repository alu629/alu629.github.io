Title: “Optimizing How We Roll” by Andrew Lu

Position Statement: Adopting cycling as their go-to mode of transportation will possibly improve the well-being of students.

Central Idea: Through utilizing cycling as their main source of transportation, the students of Baruch can avoid dealing with the obsolete MTA, improve their health and the environment, and become more independent.

 

INTRODUCTION

Attention-getter: Personal story about commuting to Baruch the morning of my presentation.

Credibility: With my background in biking and additional research, I am aware of the benefits and costs of biking.

Audience Motivation: We can change to a healthier living style by changing our method of transportation.

Purpose: Urge my audience to adopt cycling as their go-to mode of transportation.

Preview: In this presentation, I will seek to address the following points:

➢     The problems that exist in our daily lives.

➢     How biking is the solution to those daily problems we encounter.

➢     The benefits and costs of biking.

 

[Transition]: As Baruch students, we have to deal with stuff that normal college students don’t have to deal with.

 

BODY

  1. There are many problems that come with being a Baruch student, problems that will only become worse as time goes on.
    1. The problems are innumerous:
      1. It means leaving oneself to the mercy of the obsolete MTA.
      2. It leads to a sedentary lifestyle brought on by long commute times and lack of opportunities to exercise.
      3. It hinders the development of independence as we are forced to rely on others.
    2. As students of Baruch, we are suffering in this system and we need to find a solution.
    3. If left untreated, these problems will become chronic and ingrained into our systems.
      1. We will grow accustomed to this sedentary lifestyle, which leads to a higher BMI and poor cardiovascular health. (Ref #9)
      2. We remain complicit in and depend upon a system that does not value our health.

 

[Transition]: It is time to seize control. But what is to be done to avoid such a horrible outcome? As a cyclist myself, I make a humble proposal for everyone to convert from the MTA to bicycles.

 

  1. The solution to all those problems aforementioned is cycling.
    1. Instead of using the obsolete MTA, bicycle from place to place.
      1. Whereas the MTA is operated by some hidden network of workers, biking is controlled by you, the individual.
      2. Since biking is controlled by yourself, the individual, it is a step towards complete autonomy from not only the MTA, but also your parents.
      3. Biking provides some much needed exercise in the everyday of our sedentary lives.
    2. Biking is made possible through city developments that focus on improving cycling accessibility and safety. (Ref #6)
      1. As seen in this bike map issued for free by the DOT, there are quite a lot of options for cyclists, from streets that have separated bike lanes to bike-exclusive bikeways. (Ref #3) (Visual #1)

 

[Transition]: “So what exactly are the benefits and costs of biking?,” you may be asking.

 

  1. The implementation of this solution reaps high benefits with relatively low costs, literally.
    1. There are many benefits to biking.
      1. You will end up saving money, regardless if you own a bike or have to buy/rent a bike.

a)      Every month, you can potentially save $127.00, the cost of a monthly pass. (Ref #11)

b)      Even if you do not have a bike and need to buy a bike or rent a CitiBike, it will be much cheaper than MTA rates. (Ref #14, #11)

      1. You’ll also be able to appreciate the more vibrant surroundings that is impossible to find in the underground. (Visual #2)
      2. You’ll be able to save the environment by reducing your carbon footprint. (Ref #5, #13)
      3. Cycling is quicker than walking. (Ref #1, #2)
    1. While the benefits are innumerous, there are costs to biking.
      1. It takes more time and more energy to bike than it takes to take the train/bus MTA. (Ref #10, #4)

a)      However, keep in mind that you have almost complete control over your bike versus your train.

      1. There is also the inherent danger in biking.

a)      Cycling is not as dangerous as people claim it to be. (Ref #12)

b)      This can be mitigated through wearing safety gear and paying attention to your surroundings. (Ref #7, #8)

    1. Imagine yourself cruising along on your bike:
      1. The fresh air against your face, the notable absence of the weird odorous smells often encountered in the subway.
      2. The colors of the season rushing past you instead of the grey darkness of the tunnels, broken occasionally by a passing train or worksite light.
      3. The adrenaline pumping through your blood as your legs pump up and down rather than remain in place on a subway/bus seat.
    2. It is time for action.
      1. For those who know how to bike, bike more often, either through riding your own bicycle or renting a City-Bike.
      2. For those who don’t, feel free to reach out to me and I will be happy to work out a way for you to learn this essential skill.

 

[Transition]: Personal branding done, that about wraps up my presentation.

 

CONCLUSION

  1. Summary: Throughout my presentation, I have addressed the problems that biking can solve, the plans that make biking possible, and how biking solves those problems.
  2. Visualize: The problems that biking solves includes a sedentary lifestyle and dealing with the MTA, which will improve your health and decrease the amount of stressors in your life, like money and train delays.
  3. Challenge: I want everyone to rethink about renewing your monthly pass and instead consider dusting off the bike hidden in your closet or going to a bike shop.
  4. Re-focus: I hope that I have convinced everyone, bikers and non-bikers alike, that improving your health and lifestyle is as easy as riding a bike.
